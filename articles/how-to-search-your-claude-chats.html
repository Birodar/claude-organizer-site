How to search your Claude.ai chat history

Find that old conversation fast, and work around Claude's title-only search.

Once you have dozens or hundreds of Claude.ai conversations, finding the right one becomes the hard part. Claude does let you search, but only by chat title, so a thread you never renamed can be almost impossible to find. Here is how to search your Claude chat history effectively and get to any conversation in seconds.

Use Claude's built-in search

There is a search box in your Claude chat list. It matches the titles of your conversations, so typing a keyword will surface any chat whose title contains it. The limitation is that it only looks at titles, not at what you actually said inside the chat.

Rename chats so search can find them

Because search is title-based, the single most effective thing you can do is give your important chats clear titles. Rename "Untitled" or vague auto-titles to something you would search for later, like "invoice template" or "onboarding email draft". A few seconds of renaming pays off every time you look for it.

Search inside a single chat with Ctrl+F

If you have the right conversation open but need a specific line, use your browser's find function, Ctrl+F (Cmd+F on Mac), to jump to any word on the page. It will not search across chats, but it is perfect for finding a detail within one long thread.

Organize and search with an extension

To find chats faster across your whole history, a browser extension helps. Claude Organizer is a free Chrome extension that lets you sort your conversations into folders, bookmark the ones you use most, and search your saved chats by title from one place. Instead of scrolling an endless list, you narrow down by folder and search, and get to the right chat quickly. It runs entirely in your browser, with no account.

Quick recap

  • Claude's search matches titles, so rename your chats clearly.
  • Use Ctrl+F to find a line inside an open conversation.
  • Use folders and bookmarks to narrow down before you search.
